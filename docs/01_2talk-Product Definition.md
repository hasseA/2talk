# 2talk.org — Product Definition

## 1. Product Name

**2talk.org**

## 2. Product Purpose

2talk.org is a private communication platform for two people who find it difficult to communicate directly.

The difficulty may come from:

* language barriers,
* emotional tension,
* shyness,
* introversion,
* misunderstanding,
* different communication styles,
* fear of confrontation,
* difficulty expressing thoughts clearly.

The purpose of 2talk is to help two people understand each other more accurately and communicate with less unnecessary conflict.

## 3. Core Idea

2talk is not a normal chat platform and not a three-person group chat.

The AI is not a third participant.

The AI acts as a communication mediator between two people.

The basic communication flow is:

```text
Participant A writes a message
↓
The AI interprets the meaning, tone, and intention
↓
The AI produces a clearer and less confrontational version
↓
Participant B receives the mediated message
```

The same process works in the opposite direction.

## 4. Role of the AI

The AI should actively support the conversation.

Its responsibilities include:

* preserving the essential meaning of each message,
* reducing unnecessary hostility,
* clarifying unclear wording,
* helping with language differences,
* recognizing emotional tension,
* preventing avoidable escalation,
* suggesting a suitable next step when needed,
* helping each participant understand what the other may be trying to communicate.

The AI should not simply repeat the original message.

For example, if someone writes:

> Fuck you. I do not want to discuss this anymore.

The other participant should not receive the same sentence unchanged.

A mediated version could be:

> The other participant appears angry and does not want to continue this discussion right now.

The system may also privately suggest:

> It may be better to acknowledge the request and allow some space before continuing.

## 5. Meaning Must Be Preserved

The AI may soften how a message is delivered, but it must not change the central meaning.

For example:

Original message:

> Leave me alone.

Acceptable mediated version:

> The other participant is asking for space and does not want to continue the conversation right now.

Unacceptable mediated version:

> The other participant is upset but may want to reconnect later.

The unacceptable version invents an intention that was never expressed.

## 6. Active Mediation

The AI should not remain passive until someone asks for help.

It should actively process every message before it is delivered.

Depending on the situation, it may:

* clarify the message,
* soften aggressive wording,
* translate the message,
* explain that the sender appears frustrated,
* recommend a pause,
* suggest a respectful response,
* point out a likely misunderstanding,
* warn that the conversation is becoming harmful.

The AI should become visibly involved only when its guidance is useful.

The conversation should still feel like communication between two people.

## 7. Intended Users

2talk may help people who:

* speak different languages,
* struggle to express emotions,
* are afraid of confrontation,
* often misunderstand each other,
* are trying to resolve a disagreement,
* are shy or socially anxious,
* prefer written communication,
* want help maintaining a respectful conversation.

Possible use cases include:

* couples,
* family members,
* friends,
* colleagues,
* strangers trying to resolve a disagreement,
* people communicating across language barriers.

## 8. What 2talk Is Not

2talk is not:

* a normal ChatGPT session,
* a three-person group chat,
* a public forum,
* a social network,
* a debate platform,
* a therapy service,
* a legal mediation service,
* an AI judge,
* an automatic reconciliation system,
* a system that decides who is right,
* a system that invents emotions or hidden intentions.

## 9. Product Principle

The central product principle is:

> Soften the delivery without falsifying the meaning.

The AI should help people communicate more safely and clearly while preserving boundaries, disagreement, rejection, anger, and requests for space when those are genuinely part of the message.

## 10. Initial Product Scope

The first version of 2talk will focus only on private AI-mediated communication between two participants.

It will not initially include:

* public discussions,
* voting,
* expert panels,
* audience participation,
* profiles,
* followers,
* payments,
* voice calls,
* video calls,
* local language models,
* multiple AI providers.

The first version should prove one thing clearly:

> Two people can communicate through 2talk, and the AI can reduce unnecessary conflict without changing what either person actually means.
